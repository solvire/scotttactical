Real (And Pervasive) Data-Mining To Virtual Reality - Is This What Oculus Is Doing?

Critical backlash against Oculus's privacy policy arrived at Capitol Hill on Thursday, when [Sen. Al Franken](https://twitter.com/alfranken) (D-Minn.) required that Oculus and its parent company Facebook answer for that data its new headset collects from virtual reality customers.

"[Oculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oculus_VR)’ creation of an immersive virtual reality experience is definitely an exciting development," Franken authored within an [open letter to Oculus Chief Executive Officer Brendan Iribe](https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/2796548-160407OculusLetter.html), "however it remains vital that you comprehend the extent that Oculus might be collecting Americans’ private information, including sensitive location data, and discussing that information with other organizations."

Now you might ask, what is Oculus asking to gather - and just how much worse could it be in comparison with other online services' EULAs?
